    • GrowthHub

    A self-improvement app that creates a more personalized experience for different users.

    Problem:

    Numerous self-improvement applications focus on aspects like goal setting and meditation, but they often lack a sense of community that can offer solace to individuals who may experience feelings of loneliness during their journey of personal growth.

    Solution:

    Develop a platform dedicated to self-improvement, providing a vibrant hub where individuals on their personal growth journey can connect with one another in real-time. This platform would enable them to share their experiences, exchange valuable tips and tricks, and ultimately feel a stronger sense of community, alleviating the sense of loneliness often associated with self-improvement endeavors.

    Research

    Competitor Analysis:

    I conducted competitor analysis for this project. As we all know, researching and identifying key features and issues from other apps is a great way to understand what is needed to create a great app. I did research on two similar self-improvement apps, Headspace and Clarity. I was able to identify what users were looking for in an app whether its UI changes or new features.

    Guerilla Research:

    With the help of some App Store reviews, user concerns made known. This gave me insightful information about each app, whether it be about the lack of UI or features. However there are some problems with this. Firstly, it is important to note that these reviews do not account for unique and holistic individual experiences, and thus can not give us insights with real depth. Regardless, this allowed us to develop a baseline understanding of pain points and gave us required information to begin developing our ideas of the app's structure and Information Architecture.

    Research Takeaways:

    User Persona:

    Through user research I was able to find common user pain points for the stackholders and summarize these findings into a user persona for the stackholder of this app!

    Ideating

    Task Flow / User Flow:

    Through a task flow I was able to map out possible screens and steps users would have take in order to succesfuly complete a task, including both signup and discover/join/post screens! with this in mind, I was able to map out possible scenerios users may take in order to complete these tasks which helped to create the final user flow document.

    Low-fidelity Wireframes:

    Once I was done completing the Low-fideltiy Wireframes and mapping out the overall layout/shape of the app, it was time to start creating the High-fidelity Wireframes.

    Final Designs

    Brand Identity:

    I chose to maintain a clean and simple UI in my self-improvement app to prevent users from feeling overwhelmed. This was done using a analogous color scheme. The Blue helps to represent trust within the app, a key user painpoint that was sought out to solve, and the green helps represent growth, peace, and calm, crucial in a self-improvement app. I also ensured that there was consistency in the color scheme and design elements throughout the app to provide a cohesive and seamless user experience. My goal for this app was to create a space where it gave off a friendly and approachable feel. With this goal in mind, pastels of greens and blues and accents of warm colours satisfied those needs. Also, to avoid the cluster of different colours, elements, and features, I opted for a very clean UI and a smaller and more controlled palette than usual. The font I chose for this app was Inter. I wanted to make GrowthHub as user friendly as possible, and by doing so, I kept a couple things in mind while choosing the font. First, I wanted to have a font that was easy to read, no matter how big or small the screen is. Secondly, Inter gives off a friendly, yet professional vibe. GrowthHub aims to welcome new users to the app and by using Inter, it was one step forward to achieving my goal.

    High-fidelity Wireframes:

    Furthermore I made sure to follow “WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) to help ensure that GrowthHubs content is accessible by everyone, regardless of disability or user device. To meet these standards, I made sure to include a color contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for all text and interactive elements.

    User Feedback

    A/B Testing:

    Through A/B Testing using a potentinal user, I was able to gather immense feedback that would help increase both user accessibility and usability leading to a greater and more impactful user-expereince!
